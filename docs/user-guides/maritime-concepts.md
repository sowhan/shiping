# Maritime Concepts

## Navigation Terminology

### Distance and Speed

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Nautical Mile (nm)** | 1,852 meters, based on Earth's circumference |
| **Knot** | 1 nautical mile per hour |
| **League** | 3 nautical miles |

### Time

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **ETA** | Estimated Time of Arrival |
| **ETD** | Estimated Time of Departure |
| **UTC** | Coordinated Universal Time (standard reference) |

### Position

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Latitude** | North-South position (0-90° N/S) |
| **Longitude** | East-West position (0-180° E/W) |
| **Heading** | Direction vessel is pointing (0-360°) |
| **Course** | Direction vessel is moving |

## Vessel Types

### Container Vessels
- Carry standardized containers (TEU)
- Range from 1,000 to 24,000+ TEU
- Use container terminals

### Tankers
- **Crude carriers**: Raw oil transport
- **Product tankers**: Refined products
- **Chemical tankers**: Specialized chemicals
- **LNG/LPG carriers**: Gas transport

### Bulk Carriers
- Dry bulk cargo (grain, coal, ore)
- Range from 10,000 to 400,000 DWT
- Use bulk terminals

### Roll-on/Roll-off (RoRo)
- Vehicles drive on/off
- Car carriers, ferries
- Specialized ramps required

## Major Shipping Routes

### Asia-Europe
- **Suez Route**: Singapore → Suez Canal → Rotterdam
- **Cape Route**: Via Cape of Good Hope
- Distance: 8,500 nm (Suez) vs 12,000 nm (Cape)

### Trans-Pacific
- Shanghai → Los Angeles
- Distance: ~5,500 nm
- 14-17 days transit

### Trans-Atlantic
- Rotterdam → New York
- Distance: ~3,500 nm
- 10-12 days transit

## Canals

### Suez Canal
- Connects Mediterranean to Red Sea
- No locks (sea-level canal)
- Max draft: 66 feet (20.1m)
- Transit time: 11-16 hours

### Panama Canal
- Connects Atlantic to Pacific
- Lock-based system
- Neo-Panamax: 366m × 49m × 15.2m draft
- Transit time: 8-10 hours

## Port Operations

### Berth Types
- **Container berth**: Gantry cranes
- **Bulk berth**: Conveyor systems
- **Tanker berth**: Pipeline connections
- **RoRo berth**: Drive-on ramps

### Port Services
- **Pilotage**: Mandatory local pilot
- **Towage**: Tug assistance
- **Bunkering**: Fuel supply
- **Stevedoring**: Cargo handling

## Cost Components

### Fuel (Bunker) Costs
- VLSFO (Very Low Sulfur Fuel Oil)
- MGO (Marine Gas Oil)
- LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas)

### Port Costs
- Pilotage fees
- Tug fees
- Port dues
- Mooring fees
- Terminal handling

### Canal Costs
- Based on vessel size/tonnage
- Suez: $200,000 - $1,000,000+
- Panama: $100,000 - $500,000+

## Weather Factors

### Seasonal Patterns
- **Monsoons**: Indian Ocean (June-September)
- **Typhoons**: Pacific (July-November)
- **Hurricanes**: Atlantic (June-November)
- **Winter storms**: North Atlantic (October-April)

### Impact on Routes
- Speed reductions
- Route deviations
- Port delays
- Safety restrictions
